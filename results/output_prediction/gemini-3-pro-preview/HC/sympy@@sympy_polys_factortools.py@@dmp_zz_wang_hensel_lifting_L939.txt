We are given a polynomial `f` in $K[x, y]$ and its univariate factors `H` in $K[x]$ obtained by evaluating $y$ at a specific point `a`. The goal is to "lift" these factors back to multivariate factors in $K[x, y]$.

        *   This is the evaluation point for $y$.
        *   Check: $f(x, -1) = x^2 - (-1)^2 = x^2 - 1$. The factors `H` match $f(x, -1)$.

2.  **Execution steps**:
    *   The algorithm lifts the factorization $f(x, -1) = (x-1)(x+1)$ to $f(x, y) = (x+y)(x-y)$.
    *   **Initialization**: `H` is converted to multivariate form constant in $y$.
        *   $h_1 = x - 1 \Rightarrow [[1], [-1]]$ (coeffs of $x$: `[1]` and `[-1]`).

        *   We solve the Diophantine equation $T_1 h_2(a) + T_2 h_1(a) = \text{error term}$.

        *   Solving leads to correction terms:
            *   For $h_1$, we add $y+1$. $(x-1) + (y+1) = x+y$.

        *   Product $(x+y)(x-y) = x^2 - y^2 = f$. Correct.
