Let's simulate step by step.

Initial `nums = [1, -9, 7, 2, 6, -3, 3]`  
Length is `7`.  
Loop runs `len(nums) - 1 = 6` times.

---

**Iteration 1 (`_ = 0`):**  
`nums.reverse()` → `[3, -3, 6, 2, 7, -9, 1]`

**Iteration 2 (`_ = 1`):**  
`nums.reverse()` → `[1, -9, 7, 2, 6, -3, 3]`  
(back to original order)

**Iteration 3 (`_ = 2`):**  
`nums.reverse()` → `[3, -3, 6, 2, 7, -9, 1]`

**Iteration 4 (`_ = 3`):**  
`nums.reverse()` → `[1, -9, 7, 2, 6, -3, 3]`

**Iteration 5 (`_ = 4`):**  
`nums.reverse()` → `[3, -3, 6, 2, 7, -9, 1]`

**Iteration 6 (`_ = 5`):**  
`nums.reverse()` → `[1, -9, 7, 2, 6, -3, 3]`

---

Since 6 is even (loop count = 6), and we start reversing on the first iteration, reversing an even number of times from the original list returns the original list. Indeed, after iteration 6, the list is back to original.

---

Final `nums` = `[1, -9, 7, 2, 6, -3, 3]`.
